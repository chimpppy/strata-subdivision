# Services and Infrastructure Context Register
Property: 4 and 4A Gardiner Road, Goulburn NSW
Prepared by: Ben
Purpose: Provide historical and technical context to support strata certification.

## 1. Plumbing and Drainage
Confirmed facts:
- Original sewer and water layouts were hand-marked (pen) onto approved building plans.
- Council-submitted diagrams show authority-side infrastructure only.
- Missing documentation includes as-executed lines from:
  - Council water meter to each dwelling.
  - Each dwelling to sewer and stormwater connections.
- Rear rainwater tank historically supplied the front dwelling via pump.
- Pump failure led to temporary removal and was subsequently replaced by the owner.
- Current configuration:
  - Tank connected only for garden irrigation.
  - Rear dwelling not connected to the tank.
- No known adverse impact on statutory water or sewer services has been identified from current owner records.

Assumptions requiring professional confirmation:
- Final certification outcomes remain dependent on licensed plumber and certifier acceptance of as-executed documentation.

## 2. Electrical Configuration
Confirmed facts:
- Front dwelling switchboard houses the electricity meter servicing the rear dwelling.
- Rear dwelling contains an internal sub-board distribution panel.
- Site photographs document visible infrastructure.

Assumptions requiring professional confirmation:
- Configuration appears consistent with existing infrastructure but requires formal confirmation from Essential Energy (Notification of Arrangements or accepted equivalent evidence pathway).

## 3. Telecommunications
Confirmed facts:
- NBN infrastructure is installed to both dwellings.
- Physical evidence has been captured via site photographs.

Assumptions requiring professional confirmation:
- Formal Letter of Practical Completion from NBN is to be obtained, or an accepted alternative evidence pathway is to be endorsed by certifier/Council if provider letter is unavailable.

## 4. Water Metering
Confirmed facts:
- A single Council water meter services both dwellings.
- A second meter present in the pit is believed to service the neighbouring property.
- Development consent does not explicitly require separate water meters.
- Water usage is intended to be managed administratively through the strata scheme.

Assumptions requiring professional confirmation:
- Council/certifier written acceptance of the single-meter arrangement should be obtained for final certification records.
